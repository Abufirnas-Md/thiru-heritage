Palaeolithic to Megalithic

The Pre-Historic Period

Palaeolithic · Attirambakkam
Madras Hand Axe Industry
Fossil of a human leg found in association with Palaeolithic implements. Achulian-type hand axes closely similar to African hand axes were discovered. This Palaeolithic tool industry, known as the "Madras Hand Axe Industry," was in existence at Attirambakkam.
Hand AxesHuman FossilsAfrican Parallels
Neolithic · Paiyampalli
Pit Dwellings & Microlithic Tools
Sixteen natural caverns inhabited by Palaeolithic folk were located in Gudiyam Hills. Microlithic tools were unearthed from Tiruttangal excavation. Pit dwellings of Neolithic people were discovered at Paiyampalli, with Chalcolithic and Iron Age implements from T. Kallupatti, Madurai district.
MicrolithsPit DwellingsCavern Sites
Megalithic · Various Sites
Burials, Cairn Circles & Iron Age
A seated human skeleton in dhyana pose unearthed from a cist at Perumbeir in Tiruvallur District. Kādukaḷ images at Cairn Circles in Tiruvannamalai. Rearing of cattle including horse known from Sanur megalithic site bone collections. Sarcophaguses and urn burials in diverse shapes were located across the region.
Cist BurialsUrn BurialsIron AgeCairn Circles
Chalcolithic · Adichanallur
4000 Artefacts & Early Metallurgy
Nearly 4,000 artefacts collected from Adichanallur in Tirunelveli District, including copper, iron, and gold objects. Bone examination revealed Armenian group ancestry, ethnically associated with Dravidians. Evidence for knowledge of gold use from the Chalcolithic period.
Gold ObjectsCopperIronDravidian Heritage
Megalithic · Kodumanal & Kovalanpottal
Trade & Crafts Emerge
Microlithic, Neolithic tools and Megalithic artefacts at Kovalanpottal. Copper square coin of Pandyan empire and ornaments also collected. Contact with Northern India evidenced by Punch Marked coins and N.B.P. ware in Megalithic urn burials and cairn circles.

Sangam Age to Medieval Period

Major Excavation Sites

⚓
Arikkamedu
A rectangular godown (45m × 11m) and granary, dyeing tubs, ring wells, Arretine ware, Amphorae, Rouletted ware, and Terra Sigileta pottery. Strong evidence of Roman trade. Earlier strata yielded Neolithic stone implements.
📍 Pondicherry State · Early Historic
🛥️
Pumpuhar
A wharf at Kilaiyur dated 316 ± 105 B.C. (C14). Buddha Vihar at Melaiyur with five chambers decorated with stucco. A water reservoir at Vanagiri (1st–2nd C. A.D.). Underwater 'U'-shaped laterite structure found 5km seaward at 76 ft depth.
📍 Nagappattinam District · 3rd C. BC
🎲
Alagankulam
Three-phase occupation from wooden-post huts to brick houses. Roman coins (4th–5th C. A.D.). Amphorae from Paris, Italy, and Europe. A rectangular dice bearing numerals one, two, and three — most outstanding find. Rouletted pottery in pink colour named after this site.
📍 Ramanathapuram District · 2nd C. BC
⚔️
Korkai
Burial urns, brick structure in east-west orientation. Inscribed pot sherds with Tamil character from 3rd C. BC to 2nd C. A.D. Iron objects like dagger, clamp, sword. NBP sherd datable to 4th–3rd C. BC. C14 dates of 785 B.C. and 805 ± 95 B.C. from hearth charcoal.
📍 Tirunelveli District · Pre-Sangam
🏛️
Kanchipuram
Continuous artefacts from pre-Christian to Vijayanagar era. 11 terracotta coin moulds (Satavahana & Pallava). Buddha Vihar and circular stupa foundation. Gold coin of Vijayanagar period. Grey ware sherd reading 'Putalatisa' — name of a Buddhist monk.
📍 Kanchipuram District · Multi-Period
💎
Kodumanal
Bead-making and iron-melting industries. Weaving and cultivation evidence. Bronze tiger inlaid with carnelian stones. Thousands of carnelian beads. Silver punch-marked coins bearing Nandipata figure. Gold and silver spiral rings found near skeletons.
📍 Periyar District · 3rd C. BC
👑
Gangaikondacholapuram
Palace of Rajendra Chola with double-wall system (55 cm gap filled with river sand for cooling). 32 granite base stones for wooden/stone pillars. Exquisite ivory and bone carvings — yali, lion, elephant figures. Porcelain wares from South East Asia (11th C. A.D.).
📍 Perambalur District · 11th C. AD
🗡️
Padavedu
Sambuvaraya period: king's palace guarded by fort wall and moat. Ring wells in every residential place. Terracotta lamps with 1, 3, 5, 7, and 9 facets. Coin moulds and glass production furnaces. Strong Saivite affiliation evidenced by Linga figurines and Bull emblem.
📍 Tiruvannamalai District · Medieval
🌿
Sendamangalam
Artefacts spanning Megalithic to later Chola period. A copper seal of Pandya emblem. Structure of Kopperunjinga, king of Kadavaraya. Terracotta images including crawling Krishna (Chola period) and a standing couple in folk art style.

International Commerce

Tamil Nadu's Global Trade Network

Archaeological evidence from Arikkamedu, Alagankulam, Korkai, and Karur demonstrates an extensive maritime trade network. Tamil merchants exported semi-precious stone beads, shell bangles, pearls, and special pottery while importing Roman amphorae, Phoenician goods, and Greek coins.

Domestic Life, Sports & Industries

Daily Life in Ancient Tamil Nadu

🏠
Architecture & Housing
Early historic: large brick houses with lime floors. Medieval: backed-brick with stucco decorations and roof tiles. Double-wall system for cooling. Wooden pillars on stone bases, drainage channels, ring wells in every residential place.
🍚
Food & Cuisine
Rice was the staple food. In dry land areas: ragi, kambu, and corn. Charred grains of ragi, corn, and kambu found at Kodumanal. During feasts, non-vegetarian food was consumed. Oil was pressed for cooking and lamp use.
🪡
Weaving & Dyeing
Dyeing tubs discovered at Karur, Arikkamedu, and Uraiyur. Terracotta spindle whorls used for spinning. Superior textile quality attracted foreign merchants. Weaving was a major industry alongside cultivation.
🎯
Sports & Recreation
Hip-hop (kabbadi-type game) prevalent in all periods — terracotta hip-hops found in almost every layer. Dice gambling evident from 4th century B.C. at Alagankulam. Chess with terracotta and bone gamesmen. Smoking habit evidenced from later periods.
💍
Ornaments & Jewellery
Common folk wore terracotta and shell bangles. Wealthy wore gold diadems, rings, ear studs, and chains of semi-precious stone beads. Crystal beads at Gangaikondacholapuram. Bronze and glass ornaments widespread.
✍️
Literacy & Script
From Megalithic period, Tamils used graffiti symbols. Standardised Tamil script in use through Sangam age. Evolved into Vatteluttu and early modern Tamil. Inscribed potsherds in virtually all early historic sites — even ordinary citizens were literate.

"The cultural heritage of Tamils was superior in all respects."

— Natana Kasinathan, Conclusion of the Dr. Balakrishna Nayar Memorial Endowment Lecture, 1995

Spiritual Life Through the Ages

Religion & Worship

🌿
Nature Worship
Earliest Tamils worshipped natural beings — trees, water, sun. Gradually shifted to anthropomorphic deities.
🏺
Mother Goddess
Mūthoḷ (Mother Goddess) images found in cairn circles (1000–500 B.C.). Tradition continued as Mariamman worship in July–August.
☸️
Buddhism
Predominant in northern Tamil Nadu up to Pumpuhar in early historic period. Vihar at Pumpuhar with individual monk rooms. Stupa at Kanchipuram (2nd C. B.C.).
🕉️
Saivism
Shiva worship as Phallus known from Padavedu. Sambuvarayas — descendants of Sambu (Lord Shiva) — strong Saivite patrons. Lingas found widely.
🦢
Vaishnavism
Vishnu as crawling Krishna venerated — evidenced at Sendamangalam excavation. Ganapati with Mushika mount at Alagarai and Tirukkampuliyur.
🔱
Jainism
Patronised alongside Buddhism, Saivism, and Vaishnavism during the historic period. Part of the pluralistic religious landscape of ancient Tamil Nadu.
